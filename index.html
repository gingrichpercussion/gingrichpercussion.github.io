About

Percussionist & Timpanist


Justin currently holds the titles of Principal Timpani / Percussion with the Indianapolis Chamber Orchestra and Percussionist with the Johnstown Symphony Orchestra. He also frequently performs with other regional orchestras, bands, and both chamber and new music ensembles. He received a Master's of Music in Percussion Performance from Carnegie Mellon University.

His summers have been spent performing in the Peninsula Music Festival in Door County, WI and the Pittsburgh Festival Opera. He has also attended the Aspen Music Festival and the Blackburn Music Academy.

He is on the Oshkosh Symphony Orchestra recording "Crazy Horse - A Symphony by John Harmon" conducted by Daniel Black. You can listen to the album on iTunes by following this link.

Justin has received both a DownBeat Best Latin Jazz Performance Award and a Percussive Arts Society's Best World Music Ensemble Award in addition to the honor of performing at the Percussive Arts Society International Convention where he performed a gyil duet with the illustrious Valerie Dee Naranjo.

Educator


Justin has been teaching and performing for over a decade! He’s played everything from rock bands to orchestras, and taught within numerous public and private schools at both the collegiate and secondary school level. He’s given one-on-one lessons to hundreds of percussionists with ages ranging from 5 to 75 years old, and he has coached groups ranging from percussion duets / ensembles to youth and collegiate orchestral and concert band percussion sections.

He focuses on mentoring students on finding intrinsic pride in their musical pursuit, how to teach themselves in the practice room, developing technique and theoretical understanding, and improving their aptitude for performance. All of which is applied to ensemble experience, solo performance, and to audition/competition preparation.

Justin maintains in-person private studios for percussion and drumset lessons at the PYCO School of Music in Wexford, PA and the Johnstonbaugh's Music Centers in Allison Park, PA. He also provides virtual lessons through his personal zoom studio, and is contracted by the Sunburst School of Music in Pittsburgh, PA to teach drumset and voice.

Lesson Program

The Instruments

You can learn orchestra and concert band percussion (snare drum, bells, xylophone, cymbals, bass drum, triangle, tambourine), drum set, marimba, hand drumming, and more!

The Students

His students have been highly successful at completing their goals of winning auditions, being accepted into state honors ensembles, winning competitions (state solo and ensemble), getting into their top choice of collegiate institutions, and becoming better percussive artists and performers.

The Process

When you’re ready, let’s schedule your free first lesson! After, we’ll choose a lesson length, time, and payment plan (monthly or weekly) and we’ll be ready to rock and roll. Look below for the affordable lesson rates.

The (Free) First Lesson

We'll discuss your history with music and make both short- and long-term goals in addition to diving into technique and music reading.

The Flexibility

Choose lesson times ranging between 30 minutes and an hour and a half. Decide whether you'd like to pay weekly or monthly. There are two in-person locations and a virtual option available.

The Referral Program

Would you like all of your percussion friends to get better, too (and get up a discount of up to 30% off your lesson rate)? Ask about the "Gingrich Percussion (Studio) Referral Program"!

Lesson Length Factors

Experience

Players just starting can begin with 30 minutes. As our workload increases, so might our lesson time!

Skill

Is not a strong determinant, as we'll shape the lessons according to your learning rate.

Age

Younger students focus better for shorter periods of time.

Aspirations

Performers, audition-takers, and competitors will benefit from longer lessons.
